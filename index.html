Download

BreatheWith runs on iOS (including Apple Watch companion features on iPhone) and Android. Install from your usual store.

App Store Google Play

If search does not show the app in your region, open the store on your device and search for BreatheWith by name.

Google Play closed testing

We run a closed test before a wide Play release—the same flow Google documents for production access (enough opted-in testers on the track for long enough). Use the same Google account everywhere: the one on your phone’s Play Store, your tester list entry, and when you tap opt-in.

  1. Join the tester list. Enter the Gmail address for your Google account below and tap Send. We add it in Play Console. If your track uses a Google Group, join that group when your admin asks.
  2. Opt in on Google Play. Open the shareable link from Play Console (button below when available), or the message Google sends you, then accept becoming a tester. Skipping this step means you are not counted as opted-in.
  3. Install from Play. After opting in, install the app from the Play Store flow for testers.

Submissions are delivered by FormSubmit to our support inbox. The first time this form is used, FormSubmit sends us a one-time confirmation link we must activate.

Frequently asked questions

How do I change the breathing rhythm?

On the main screen, tap the Settings (gear) icon. Under Breathing Rhythm, pick a preset such as 4–6 Calm, 5–5 Balance, or Box Breathing.

Where are volume and haptic controls?

Audio and haptics live on the Settings screen. Tap the gear icon, then open Audio & Haptics to adjust the metronome or toggles.

Does the app work on Apple Watch?

Yes. BreatheWith includes a companion experience for Apple Watch that works with the iPhone app, using haptics to guide your breath on your wrist.

Sound or haptics stopped working — what should I try?

Confirm the device is not in Silent Mode (iPhone) or that media volume is up (Android). In the app, revisit Audio & Haptics and ensure features are enabled. On Watch, check that haptics are allowed in system settings.

Is there a subscription or data tracking?

No. BreatheWith is built for privacy: no analytics SDKs in the app as described in our policy, and your practice data stays on your device. Pricing (one-time purchase or free) depends on region and store listing.

How do I join the Android closed test on Google Play?

On Google Play closed testing, submit your Gmail with the form, then use the opt-in link when we publish it or in Google’s email. Install from Play after you accept. See Google’s testing guide.
